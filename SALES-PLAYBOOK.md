# QuoteFollow — Sales Playbook

## Objection Handling

### "I already follow up with my quotes"
**Response:** "That's great — you're ahead of most contractors. But here's the thing: studies show it takes 5-7 touchpoints to close a deal. Most contractors stop at 1-2. We do 4 over 18 days, professionally, so you can focus on the work."

### "I can do this myself"
**Response:** "You absolutely can. But are you? Most contractors intend to follow up but get busy on the job site. We make sure it happens every single time, consistently. What's your time worth per hour? $50? $100? We save you 5-10 hours a month for $149."

### "It's too expensive"
**Response:** "Let's do the math: if you send 30 quotes a month and your average job is $3,000, increasing your close rate from 20% to 30% means 3 more jobs = $9,000 more revenue. We cost $149. That's a 60x return."

### "I don't trust email follow-ups"
**Response:** "Totally fair. That's why we use a multi-touch approach — email plus a final phone call. And every message is personalized to the specific job, not a generic template. Want to see a sample?"

### "My customers will think I'm being pushy"
**Response:** "We hear this a lot. The key is how you do it. Our follow-ups are helpful, not pushy. We reference the specific job, address common concerns, and give them an easy way to say yes or no. Most customers appreciate the attention."

## Call Script

**Opening:** "Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. I saw you run [Trade] in [City] — how's business been?"

**Discovery:**
- "How many quotes do you typically send per month?"
- "What's your close rate on those quotes?"
- "How many times do you follow up on a quote that doesn't get a response?"
- "What happens to quotes that don't close? Do you revisit them?"

**Pitch:**
"Here's what we do: you send us your quotes — name, email, job details — and we handle the follow-ups. Four touches over 18 days. Professional, personalized, and consistent. You focus on the jobs, we make sure no quote falls through the cracks."

**Close:**
"Look, I know this is new. So here's what I suggest: send me your 5 most recent quotes that haven't closed. I'll run the sequence on them for free. If it doesn't get at least one response, you never hear from me again. Fair?"
